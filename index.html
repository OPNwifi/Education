Understanding Wi-Fi Threats

Wi-Fi attacks happen when hackers exploit weaknesses in wireless networks, often in public places. This section will explain the most common types of attacks, including Man-in-the-Middle, Evil Twin, and more.

Tips to Protect Yourself

Connecting to open Wi-Fi networks can expose your sensitive data to attackers. Learn about the risks and how to stay safe by using VPNs, turning off automatic Wi-Fi connections, and checking for secure sites.

What is Wi-Fi?

Wi-Fi stands for "Wireless Fidelity" and allows devices like smartphones, laptops, and tablets to connect to the internet without using cables. It works by sending data over radio waves between your device and a router, which connects to the internet. While it's very convenient, using public or unprotected Wi-Fi can expose your personal information to hackers.

What is an IP Address?

An IP address (Internet Protocol address) is a unique number assigned to every device that connects to the internet. Think of it like a home address, but for your devices online. Just as the post office uses your home address to deliver mail, the internet uses your IP address to send information to your device. It's important to keep your IP address private because hackers can use it to track your online activities or even launch attacks.

About

This website is part of a senior design project at Michigan Tech. The purpose of this project is to spread awareness about the vulnerabilities associated with using open Wi-Fi networks. Through educational videos, detailed information, and interactive games, this site aims to inform users about the dangers of connecting to public networks and how they can protect their data.

Open Wi-Fi networks, such as those in coffee shops, airports, or libraries, are often unsecured, making them prime targets for hackers. Our goal is to educate the public on these risks and provide actionable tips to enhance their security. This project reflects Michigan Tech's commitment to cybersecurity education and real-world solutions.
